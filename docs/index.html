The Problem: Legal AI Hallucinations Are Dangerous

AI language models confidently fabricate case citations, misstate holdings, and invent court precedents — a phenomenon with documented real-world consequences. 486 court cases have involved AI-generated hallucinations in legal filings, leading to sanctions, dismissed motions, and reputational damage.

Attorneys cannot manually verify every AI-generated citation in a 50-page brief. VerifAI automates that verification loop — flagging suspicious claims before they reach a courtroom.

How It Works

VerifAI runs a 4-stage pipeline on any AI-generated legal text:

✂️

1. Claim Extraction

GPT-4o decomposes text into atomic, independently verifiable claims with structured JSON output.

🔎

2. Evidence Retrieval

Each claim is searched against CourtListener's 5M+ court opinions via REST API (RAG — no local corpus).

⚖️

3. NLI Scoring

DeBERTa-v3 scores entailment between each claim and its evidence. Citation existence claims get a hard-check lookup.

📋

4. Transparency Report

Color-coded Streamlit report with per-claim verdicts: SUPPORTED, CONTRADICTED, or INSUFFICIENT EVIDENCE.
